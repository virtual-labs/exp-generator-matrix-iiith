Instructions
    Quick theory overview:
  • A linear block code is a \(k\)-dimensional subspace of the vector space \(\mathbb{F}_2^n\). Let \(\mathbf{g}_1, \mathbf{g}_2, \ldots, \mathbf{g}_k\) be a basis of this subspace. Then the generator matrix \(G \in \mathbb{F}_2^{k \times n}\) corresponding to this basis is given by \begin{align*} % G= \begin{bmatrix} \leftarrow & \mathbf{g}_1 & \rightarrow \\ \leftarrow & \mathbf{g}_2 & \rightarrow \\ & \vdots & \\ \leftarrow & \mathbf{g}_k & \rightarrow \end{bmatrix}. \end{align*}

    Procedure:
  • Initially, all the vectors are in grey color.
  • Clicking on a vector changes its color from grey to green. All the vectors with green background indicate that a vector is selected.
  • To deselect a vector, click on it again. Its color changes back to grey.
  • Select 3 codewords one by one to form the rows of the generator matrix and click on Submit.
  • Observations section displays whether a valid generator matrix is selected or not.
  • For systematic generator matrix, the order in which the codewords are selected is important.
  • Reset - Deselects all the vectors and brings it back to the initial state.
